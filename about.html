About Us

Mission

At Excell Water Works, our mission is to assist our customers to transform the city water they receive through their taps into a crystal-clear, pure tasting elixir, when they use our sustainable and powerful natural filter - Excell Works activated bamboo charcoal.

Our Vision

Our Customers do not have to buy bottled water anymore. Now they can use our activated bamboo charcoal to purify their own tap water for drinking, cooking, and cleaning, knowing that it is free of harmful chemicals and bacteria. We are committed to providing our customers with the best possible service and support, and we are constantly striving to improve our products and processes to ensure that we are always at the forefront of the industry.

Our History

In 1838, Edward Samuels walked away from the plantation in Westmoreland, following his father's horse to a 40-acre property in the hills of the Donalva District in Hanover, known as "Barrel".

The subsequent generation, comprising nine sons and a daughter, thrived through yam and ground provision farming, expanding the property to 240 acres. However, a near tragedy altered the course, reducing the holding to 140 acres. The third generation sustained yam farming, but the fourth generation migrated to different lands and trades. Upon their return, the land was overrun with Bambusa vulgaris previously used as yam sticks for yam support, highligting the need to address the bamboo proliferation with urgency.

In 2019, Donald Excell visited the Bureau of Standards(BSJ), engaging in a discussion with Mr. Gladstone Rose, then the Director of Special Projects, about bamboo's uses, particularly in producing bamboo charcoal as a tree-friendly alternative for protecting the environment. During the meeting, Mr. Rose proposed focusing on non-chemical methods for activated bamboo charcoal production.

The family conducted persistent research, leading to the production of BBQ Charcoal, later named and branded as "More Fire" bamboo charcoal because of its unique properties. By December 2023, the family achieving successful activation of bamboo charcoal verified by Laboratory analyses after submitting samples to the BSJ on two prior occasions. The activated bamboo charcoal was named "Charboo" and the process called "Excell Works" in honor of the family's main researcher, Donald Excell.
